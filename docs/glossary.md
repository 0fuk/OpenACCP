# Glossary

- `accepted evidence`: evidence that final authority has reviewed and accepted. Analogy: a signed-off inspection record.
- `assumption ledger`: visible assumptions with evidence, risk, and expiry. Analogy: a list of bets the team is knowingly making.
- `authority charter`: explicit permission for a role to act within limits. Analogy: a temporary work permit.
- `B0`: read-only discovery and review. Look, compare, and report.
- `B1`: packaging and preparation. Turn gaps into task cards, matrices, and worker-ready material.
- `B2`: scoped execution. Do the named work under a task card and charter.
- `B3`: final authority. Accept, waive, merge, release, publish, or make another binding final decision.
- `Frontier`: lane orchestrator. It manages a bounded area of work and prepares or dispatches scoped tasks when chartered.
- `handoff`: structured evidence from a role. Analogy: a delivery note that says what changed, what was checked, what remains, and what cannot be claimed.
- `Primary`: final-authority role. It consumes reviewed evidence and owns binding decisions.
- `reviewer`: read-only sidecar reviewer. It checks scope, tests, claims, and residual risk.
- `source pack`: current fact base. Analogy: the folder of documents everyone agrees to read first.
- `task card`: smallest safe executable unit. It tells a worker what to do, what not to do, how to verify, and when to stop.
- `validator`: structure and hygiene checker. It catches missing fields and common unsafe claims, but it is not a judge of product correctness.
- `worker`: scoped executor. It performs the task card and returns a handoff.
